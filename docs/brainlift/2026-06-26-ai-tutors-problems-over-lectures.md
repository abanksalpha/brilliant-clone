# Educational Apps Should Not Be Fun

**Topic:** AI-powered learning tools (AP Physics C tutoring). Adam Banks, Alpha AI Engineering, 2026-06-26.

## DOK 1 - Facts

1. Deslauriers et al. (2019): in a randomized college physics study with identical
   materials, active-learning students scored higher but felt they learned less.
   "Actual learning and feeling of learning were strongly anticorrelated."
2. Soderstrom & Bjork (2015): performance (what we observe during study) is "often
   an unreliable index" of learning (durable retention and transfer); some
   manipulations move the two in opposite directions.
3. Roediger & Karpicke (2006): retrieval practice beat restudying on a delayed
   test, even though learners predicted the opposite.
4. Dunlosky et al. (2013): of ten study techniques, practice testing and spacing
   rate high utility; rereading, highlighting, and summarizing rate low, yet are
   the most commonly used.
5. Kirschner, Sweller & Clark (2006): for novices, explicit instruction with worked
   examples beats minimal guidance.

## DOK 2 - Summary

Smooth performance and the feeling of "getting it" are not learning. Learning is a
durable change that shows up later, on delayed unaided tests, and it comes from
effortful retrieval, spacing, and worked examples, not fluent review. Learners
mistake ease for mastery, so the activities that feel best often teach least. The
fix is not less teaching, but the right kind: worked examples plus effortful
practice.

## DOK 3 - Insight

Map this onto a product and the design implication inverts the industry's. Since
ease is a false signal and durable learning requires desirable difficulties, the
worst move is redesigning the experience to feel better, whether as a fluent
interactive lecture or as gamified delight; "feels better" usually means the
difficulty that teaches was removed. The constructive move, which no single source
states, is the opposite: keep the experience almost identical to doing the work by
hand, and aim AI at efficiency, not experience. Its leverage is stripping out dead
time and blind spots, grading handwritten reasoning in seconds instead of days,
scheduling retrieval just as memory fades, and picking the next problem to hit the
exact misconception you revealed. The best app should feel almost exactly like
normal work, with the waste removed.

## DOK 4 - Spiky Point of View

**Educational apps should stop trying to be fun: because the feeling of learning is
anticorrelated with learning, the best app feels almost exactly like working through
the material by hand and spends its AI on making that effortful process far more
efficient, not more enjoyable.**
